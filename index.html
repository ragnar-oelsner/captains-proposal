The Concept

Own the boat once. Stop paying for charters forever.

The math is simple: we each buy a solid 35–38-foot offshore sloop for $15,000–$25,000. Instead of paying $3,000–$8,000 per person to charter someone else's boat for a week, we own ours — and everyone who sails chips in $100–$150 a day toward fuel and costs. That's a two-week Caribbean trip for around $1,200–$1,500 per person, sleeping aboard, eating aboard, no hotel, no charter company taking 40% off the top.

The boats live in Oyster Bay for the summer — moored right off Centre Island for free — then head south to Palm Beach in October. Free anchorage at Lake Worth or Peanut Island all winter. From Palm Beach it's 55 miles across the Gulf Stream to the Bahamas, and from there the Caribbean is open.

Why Ownership Beats Chartering

A bareboat charter in the BVI runs $3,000–$7,000 per boat per week — before provisioning, fuel, or flights. You pay that every time, own nothing, and get whatever's available. Buy for $20K and by year three you've already broken even on that first trip. Every sail after that costs less than any charter you'd book — and you're on your own boat.

The Cost-Sharing Model

Each boat owner charges crew a daily contribution — roughly $100–$150/person/day — covering provisioning, fuel, dockage fees, and a portion of the annual ownership cost. At 5 people per boat and 14 days, crew contributions fully cover the trip costs and chip in ~$2,500–$3,500 toward annual ownership. Trips pay for themselves.

The Centre Island Base

The entire economics work because of the free mooring. Outdoor shower, grill, sauna, lounge, beachfront dining, gym, truck access — everything you need ashore is already there. The boat is moored right off Montecito Drive, east of Seawanhaka. Free dockage in New York changes the math dramatically.

Three Boats Changes Everything

One boat is a weekend toy. Three boats is a completely different thing. You coordinate trips, sail together, raft up at anchor. In the Bahamas, three boats means 12–15 people in the same anchorage — everyone's on each other's cockpits for cocktail hour, dinners rotate between boats, someone's always fishing off the stern. That's the trip people talk about for the rest of their lives.

The Vessel

A classic offshore 35–38-foot sloop.

The target is a proven bluewater monohull from the 1980s — when American and European yards were building serious boats for serious ocean work. These hulls were designed to go offshore, not putt around a marina. Heavy, stiff, seaworthy, and priced at a fraction of anything built in the last 20 years.

The Mooring Setup

A single helix or mushroom-anchor mooring installed once for $1,500–$2,500, maintained for a few hundred dollars a year. Town of Oyster Bay issues mooring permits — waterfront owners typically have access. Confirm with the Bay Constable before committing to the plan.

The Annual Calendar

A full year on the water — north and south.

The plan runs on two axes: a New York home base from May through September, and a Palm Beach/Caribbean base from October through April. The snowbird run connects them. The Caribbean season is when the fleet does its big trips together.

The Snowbird Run — Oyster Bay to Palm Beach

~1,200 nm south over 3–4 weeks. Route: Oyster Bay → offshore NJ coast → past the Chesapeake entrance → Norfolk (ICW Mile 0) → Beaufort NC → Charleston → Savannah → St. Augustine → West Palm Beach. Runs offshore where possible to avoid the Chesapeake detour — saves 3–4 days.

Every stop is worth the time: Beaufort NC, Charleston, Savannah, St. Augustine. Budget $3,500–$6,000 for the passage (fuel, occasional dockage, provisions).

Palm Beach Base — Lake Worth & Peanut Island

Anchor free at Lake Worth or Peanut Island — two of the most established free anchorages on the East Coast. Protected water, easy dinghy access to West Palm Beach, PBI airport nearby for fly-in visits. This is the weekend-and-holidays base. Thanksgiving on the hook. Cost: $0 in dockage.

🐚

The Caribbean Season — Bahamas, BVI, St. Barts

All three boats depart West Palm Beach in a weather window. 55 miles across the Gulf Stream to West End, Grand Bahama — a 4–6 hour passage in settled weather. From there: the Abacos, Nassau, Exumas. Friends rotate in and out by flight.

Longer legs extend east and south: Turks & Caicos → Puerto Rico → USVI → BVI → St. Barts. These are separate 2–3 week expeditions for those with more time. The boats can also be positioned and crew flies to meet them.

The Cayman Islands (Optional — Western Route)

The Caymans sit south of Cuba and west of Jamaica — a different direction from the Bahamas/BVI route. Best approach from the US is via Cuba's southern coast or from Belize/Mexico. This is a more serious offshore passage (450–500 nm from Florida around Cuba) and works best as a dedicated 3–4 week trip. Friends with a property in the Caymans make this a natural destination stop. Most practically done with one or two boats, crew flying in and out.

The Crew Rotation Model

On any Caribbean trip, the three boats carry 12–15 people total. Ragnar, Max, and Zack are aboard the full trip. Friends rotate in and out by flight — fly into Nassau, George Town, San Juan, or Grand Cayman, sail for 5–10 days, fly home. The boats and captains stay; the crew is whoever can make the dates work. That's how you run a 15-person trip without 15 people all taking the same three weeks off.

The Path Forward

Five steps to making this real.

1. Confirm the mooring permit

Call Oyster Bay Town Hall / Bay Constable's office. Confirm a mooring permit is available for your waterfront on Montecito Drive. This is the keystone of the whole plan — if the mooring doesn't work, everything else changes.

2. Find the boat

Search YachtWorld, BoatTrader, and Cruisers Forum classifieds. Target 35–38-foot offshore sloops under $25,000. Narrow to boats with: low engine hours, recent standing rigging, working autopilot, and a Yanmar diesel. Have a surveyor inspect before signing.

3. Pre-purchase survey

Non-negotiable. Budget $900–$1,200 for a marine surveyor plus sea trial. Two things to ask explicitly: when was standing rigging last replaced, and what is the actual engine hour count. These two items determine whether the $15K boat is actually a $15K boat or a $25K boat.

4. Year-one upgrades

Once the boat is in hand: install 200–400W solar, replace batteries if needed, get a reliable dinghy + 5hp outboard, add a cellular bilge alarm (Siren Marine or BoatCommand), and verify all nav electronics. Budget $5,000–$8,000. This is what makes the anchor-out lifestyle reliable.

5. Set the first group date

Pick a weekend in August — Block Island or Nantucket — and commit it. Max and Zack need a real date to aim toward, not an open-ended intention. Once three boats are pointed at the same anchorage on the same weekend, the Captains fleet is real. The Bahamas trip books itself from there.

HOME WATERS — SUMMER

🗽 Long Island Sound — Montauk & Eastern LI

🎣 ★★★ 🪁 ★★ 🏝️ ★★ 🎉 ★★

Fishing

Montauk is one of the premier bluefish, striped bass, and tuna fishing grounds on the entire East Coast. The Canyon runs (offshore, 60–70 miles) produce yellowfin, bigeye, and bluefin tuna in summer. Inshore: striper and bluefish every evening along the beach. Anchor near the lighthouse — fish come to you.

Kiteboarding & Wind

Long Island Sound is medium wind in summer — enough for great sailing, not a dedicated kite destination. Kiteboarding is better at Jones Beach, Shinnecock Bay (Hampton Bays), and the outer bays. The Sound itself is better for daysailing and racing. Expect 10–18 knots on most summer afternoons.

Untouched Anchorages & Nature

  • Lake Montauk — protected inner harbor, anchor for free, dinghy to town
  • Shelter Island — two-boat anchorage, quiet, beautiful
  • Gardiners Island — private but circumnavigate, fantastic wildlife
  • Plum Island — anchor off, bird sanctuary, striking geology

Nightlife & Grilling

Montauk has gone serious nightlife — Gurney's, the Surf Lodge, Navy Beach. Sag Harbor is quieter and excellent. The move is: grill on the boat at anchor during the day, dinghy ashore for dinner and drinks. Don't stay in a marina just to walk to bars — anchor and use the dinghy.

Best window: June – September · Striped bass peak: Sept–Oct · Tuna canyon runs: July–August
130 NM FROM OYSTER BAY

🌿 Block Island, Rhode Island

🎣 ★★ 🪁 ★★★ 🏝️ ★★★ 🎉 ★★

Kiteboarding & Wind

Block Island is one of the best kiteboarding spots in the Northeast. The island is exposed on all sides — consistent 15–25 knot winds most of the season. The southeast coast (Fred Benson Town Beach area) is the primary kite zone. Water is flat in the channel behind the island. Serious kite destination.

Untouched Nature

40% of the island is preserved natural land — the Southeast Lighthouse bluffs, the Mohegan Bluffs (200-foot clay cliffs), and the North and South Ponds are genuinely spectacular and wild. Rent bikes on the island, hike the trails, anchor in Great Salt Pond and walk out to the bluffs.

Fishing

  • Striped bass trolling around the island September–October
  • Bluefish from the boat at anchor all summer
  • Fluke in the Great Salt Pond
  • Offshore: Block Island Canyon for tuna in July/August

Nightlife & Scene

The Great Salt Pond anchorage fills with 500+ boats on summer weekends — the biggest floating social scene in New England. Dinghies rafting up, paddleboards, music carrying across the water. Ashore: The Oar and the Mahogany Shoals are the classics. Not Miami nightlife — more like a very good summer beach party with great people.

Best window: June – September · Kite season: July–September · Peak anchorage crowd: July 4th & Labor Day weekends
180 NM FROM OYSTER BAY

⛵ Nantucket, Massachusetts

🎣 ★★ 🪁 ★★ 🏝️ ★★ 🎉 ★★★

The Vibe

Nantucket is the prestige stop — cobblestones, cedar-shake houses, good restaurants, an actual party scene in summer. Anchor in Madaket on the west end for quiet, or pick up a town mooring for quick access to the main action. The Nantucket Boat Basin has a famous dock scene on summer evenings.

Nature & Beaches

Great Point on the island's north tip is a seal colony and shore-fishing destination. Coskata-Coatue Wildlife Refuge is one of the great undeveloped barrier spits on the East Coast. Rent bikes and explore the moors — the interior of Nantucket looks like Scotland.

Fishing

  • Great Point surf fishing: legendary striped bass spot
  • Bluefish around the shoals all summer
  • Bonito and false albacore in September — speed fish, light tackle
  • Nantucket Sound: fluke and scup year-round

Nightlife & Dining

The Chicken Box for live music and late nights. The Gazebo and Lola 41 for cocktails and crowd. Ventuno for Italian. Topper's for serious dining. For the boat — buy lobsters from the Nantucket Lobster Trap ($12–$15/lb), boil them in the cockpit with corn and butter. Best meal of the summer, every time.

Best window: July – September · Peak nightlife: July 4th, Daffodil Festival (April), Christmas Stroll (December)
SNOWBIRD RUN — OCTOBER

🦀 Chesapeake Bay — Annapolis & Beyond

🎣 ★★★ 🪁 ★ 🏝️ ★★★ 🎉 ★★

Untouched Anchorages — The Best in the US

The Chesapeake has more protected, beautiful, completely free anchorages than any body of water in the country. Solomons Island. Smith Island (inhabited, no cars, time-stopped). Tangier Island. Fishing Creek. The Miles River. You can spend a week anchoring in a different cove every night and never see another boat.

Fishing

  • Blue crab — drop a pot off the stern at anchor, haul it every morning. Cook aboard.
  • Striped bass (rockfish) — October is peak season, the best striped bass fishing on the East Coast
  • Oysters — harvest from sanctioned beds (check regulations), eat raw at anchor
  • Bluefish — blitzing schools all fall

Grilling & Nature

The Chesapeake in October is a grilling paradise. Anchor in a quiet cove. Pull up blue crabs from the pot. Grill rockfish on the cockpit grill. Osprey, bald eagles, and herons everywhere. The light in October on the Bay is extraordinary — flat water, golden afternoons, cool nights. Bring sweatshirts.

Annapolis Stop

The sailing capital of the US. Historic downtown, excellent restaurants, the Naval Academy waterfront. Ego Alley is a famous channel where boats parade in — watch from a dinghy. The United States Sailboat Show in October is the world's largest in-water boat show. Time your southbound trip to stop here for a week.

Best window for snowbird stop: mid-September – November · Rockfish peak: October · Crab season: May–November
ICW STOP — SOUTH CAROLINA

🌺 Charleston, South Carolina

🎣 ★★ 🪁 ★ 🏝️ ★★ 🎉 ★★★

The Stop to Not Skip

Charleston is one of the finest small cities in America — extraordinary food, beautiful historic architecture, a social scene that punches well above its size. Anchor in the Ashley River, dinghy to the marina, walk into town. Budget at least 3–4 nights here on the southbound ICW run.

Nearby Nature

  • Capers Island — uninhabited barrier island, camping permitted, incredible shells and birds
  • ACE Basin — one of the largest undeveloped estuaries on the East Coast, kayaking and wildlife
  • Bull Island — wilderness beach, no development, alligators in the marsh, no crowds
  • Cape Romain NWR — accessible only by boat

Food & Nightlife

FIG, Husk, The Ordinary (great seafood bar), Leon's — Charleston restaurant quality rivals NYC. King Street for late nights. The Rooftop at Vendue. Charleston is genuinely one of the best restaurant cities in the US — eat well here, provisions for the next leg, move south.

Fishing

Inshore: redfish, trout, flounder in the estuaries. Crabbing in the marsh creeks. Offshore: dolphin (mahi), king mackerel, amberjack when conditions allow. The Gulf Stream is accessible from Charleston for a full offshore day trip.

Best passage timing: October–November (southbound), April (northbound)
55 NM FROM PALM BEACH — GULF STREAM CROSSING

🐠 The Bahamas — Abacos & Exumas

🎣 ★★★ 🪁 ★★★ 🏝️ ★★★ 🔥 ★★★ 🎉 ★★

Untouched Anchorages

  • Warderick Wells Cay — Exuma Land & Sea Park, no fishing inside, clearest water in the Bahamas, completely pristine. Anchor in the blue hole.
  • Staniel Cay — swimming pigs, Thunderball Grotto (James Bond), nurse sharks. Anchor, snorkel, barbecue on the beach.
  • Shroud Cay — uninhabited, tidal creeks, camp on the beach, no phone signal
  • Allan's Cay — iguana colony, anchor alone, nothing else there

Fishing

  • Mahi-mahi on the Gulf Stream crossing — troll en route, arrive with dinner
  • Wahoo — fast, excellent eating, Exumas chain in winter
  • Bonefish — the premier flats fishing in the world. Andros Island is the bonefishing capital. Hire a guide, or sight-cast from the dinghy on the flats at low tide.
  • Spearfishing — grouper, snapper, hogfish (bring pole spear)
  • Lobster — Bahamian crawfish (spiny lobster), legal to free-dive for

Kiteboarding

The Bahamas is a world-class kiteboarding destination. Long Island (Bahamas) has consistent trade winds and flat sand flats on the western side. Eleuthera's Surfer's Beach is a kite destination. The Abacos have kite-friendly conditions on the banks side. Bring gear.

Grilling & Party Stops

  • Staniel Cay Yacht Club — burger night, Kalik on the dock, bonfire on the beach. Unpretentious and legendary.
  • Hopetown, Abacos — candy-stripe lighthouse, the Abaco Inn, small community with a great bar scene
  • Nassau — the only actual city. Arawak Cay fish fry for conch salad and Kalik beer. Not a party destination but a great provisioning and culture stop.
  • Any deserted beach: anchor the boat, dinghy ashore, build a fire, grill mahi caught that morning. This is the move.
Best window: November – April (northeast trade winds, no hurricane season) · Bonefish peak: year-round · Lobster season: August 1 – March 31
+300 NM SOUTHEAST OF NASSAU

🌊 Turks & Caicos

🎣 ★★★ 🪁 ★★★ 🏝️ ★★★ 🎉 ★★

Kiteboarding — World-Class

Long Bay Beach on Providenciales is one of the premier kiteboarding beaches in the world. Consistent 15–25 knot trades from the northeast, flat warm water on the banks side, a dedicated kite community. Fly in just for this if you need to — or arrive by boat and stay a week.

Untouched Cays

  • French Cay — remote shark sanctuary, nurse and reef sharks everywhere, snorkeling is extraordinary
  • West Caicos — uninhabited, pristine wall diving, anchor in the northwest bay
  • Middle Caicos — largest island, barely visited, limestone caves, deserted beaches
  • South Caicos — working conch fishing village, no tourists, genuine Caribbean

Fishing

  • Bonefish on the flats around North Caicos and Middle Caicos
  • Blue marlin and yellowfin tuna offshore — Caicos Bank drops off to deep water quickly
  • Conch — everywhere, pull them off the bottom in the shallows, make conch salad at anchor
  • Wahoo trolling between the islands

Scene

Provo (Providenciales) has Turks Kite Club, the Hole in the Wall bar, and Grace Bay for a nice dinner. The island has gentrified into a luxury destination but the outer cays are completely untouched. Arrive by boat, anchor at French Cay, come into Provo for provisions and a good meal, go back out. That's the right approach.

Best window: December – April · Kite season peaks: January–March · Trade winds most consistent: Feb–April
BRITISH VIRGIN ISLANDS / US VIRGIN ISLANDS

⛵ BVI & USVI — The Classic

🎣 ★★ 🪁 ★★ 🏝️ ★★★ 🔥 ★★★ 🎉 ★★★

Must-Do Anchorages

  • The Bight, Norman Island — anchor in clear water, dinghy to William Thornton ("Willy T") floating bar, snorkel Treasure Cave at the headland
  • The Baths, Virgin Gorda — giant granite boulders forming sea pools, one of the most photographed spots in the Caribbean
  • Anegada — flat coral atoll, endless lobster, bonefish flats, Cow Wreck Beach Bar, pink flamingos. The BVI's best-kept secret.
  • Sandy Spit, Green Cay — tiny deserted sandbar island. Anchor off, swim ashore. Paradise.

Party Stops

  • Jost Van Dyke — Soggy Dollar Bar: invented the Painkiller cocktail. Arrive by dinghy, anchor off White Bay. One of the most famous beach bars in the world.
  • Willy T (William Thornton): floating bar on Norman Island. Famous for jumping off the top deck.
  • Foxy's, Jost Van Dyke: legendary New Year's Eve party draws thousands of boats
  • St. John (USVI): Cruz Bay — excellent bars, 60% National Park, stunning hiking

Fishing & Nature

  • Anegada has the best bonefish flats in the eastern Caribbean
  • Deep-sea: blue marlin, wahoo, mahi trolling between islands
  • Spearfishing for grouper and snapper (check BVI regulations)
  • Dolphins bow-riding the boat on every passage between islands
  • Turtle nesting beaches on Anegada and Peter Island

The Grilling Move

Anegada Reef Hotel hosts a lobster barbecue most evenings — full lobster, rice, and salad on a beach. But the better version: dinghy to shore, buy live lobsters from local fishermen, take them back to the boat, split and grill over charcoal in the cockpit, eat at sunset while anchored off a deserted beach. This happens every other night in the BVI if you want it to.

Best window: November – May · New Year's Eve: book mooring balls at Jost Van Dyke months in advance · Trade winds peak Jan–March
+50 NM FROM BVI

🥂 St. Barts — Gustavia

🎣 ★★ 🪁 ★★ 🏝️ ★★ 🎉 ★★★

The Scene

St. Barts is the French Riviera of the Caribbean — Gustavia harbor fills with superyachts, the restaurants are legitimately excellent, the beaches are beautiful, and the nightlife in January–March is as good as the Caribbean gets. Anchor out in Gustavia harbor (it's free) and watch the superyacht parade from the cockpit. Dinner at Le Tamarin or L'Isoletta ashore.

Beaches

  • Colombier Beach — only accessible by boat or 45-min hike. Anchor off, swim ashore. Completely undeveloped. One of the best beaches in the Caribbean.
  • Shell Beach — right in Gustavia, easy dinghy access, covered in shells
  • Saline Beach — clothing-optional, wild, no facilities, stunning
  • Gouverneur — quiet, gorgeous, anchor off the south coast

New Year's Eve

Gustavia harbor on New Year's Eve is one of the great parties in the Caribbean — boats rafted three-deep, fireworks, music from every boat, French champagne flowing. Book your spot in the harbor early. This is worth building the trip calendar around.

Practical Notes

St. Barts is French territory — check in is straightforward. Gustavia has excellent provisioning (Marché U supermarket), duty-free alcohol (stock up here — the best prices in the Caribbean), and chandlery supplies. The harbor is well-protected. Plan 4–5 nights minimum — it earns it.

Best window: December – March · New Year's Eve: book months ahead · High season (busy, expensive): Christmas–mid-January
WESTERN ROUTE — VIA CUBA SOUTH COAST

🏝️ Cayman Islands — Grand Cayman, Little Cayman, Cayman Brac

🎣 ★★★ 🪁 ★★ 🏝️ ★★★ 🎉 ★★

Why Go

The Caymans are one of the last truly world-class Caribbean destinations that isn't overrun. Grand Cayman's Seven Mile Beach is legitimately one of the best in the world — calm, clear, warm, no seagrass, long and wide. Little Cayman and Cayman Brac are remote and extraordinary. Friends with property on-island make this a real home-port stop.

Diving & Fishing

  • Little Cayman — Bloody Bay Wall: ranked among the world's top 5 dive sites. 6,000-foot wall starts at 20 feet. Bring dive gear or hire a boat.
  • Stingray City: famous sandbar where wild stingrays feed from your hand. Snorkel or dive.
  • Deep-sea fishing: blue marlin, wahoo, mahi. The Cayman Trench drops to 25,000 feet — deep-water species close to shore.
  • Bonefish: North Sound flats on Grand Cayman

Getting There

The route from Palm Beach goes via the Bahamas, then west along Cuba's south coast, then south to Grand Cayman — roughly 600 nm total. The Cuba passage requires advance planning (permission, routing) and is a serious offshore leg. The alternative: position the boat in Mexico/Belize and approach from the west. Or: fly crew into Grand Cayman from anywhere in the world while the boat stays in the Bahamas.

Scene & Friends

Georgetown (Grand Cayman) has good restaurants and a lively expat scene, but the island is more low-key than St. Barts or BVI. The real draw is the water and the diving, not the nightlife. Having friends with property here is the key — that turns a marina stop into a home base and opens up the island in a way tourists never see.

Best window: November – April (dry season) · Hurricane season ends: November · Stingray City: year-round

Are you in?

One boat is a weekend toy. Three boats is a completely different thing. The Bahamas trip alone is worth buying in — and every year after that costs less.
